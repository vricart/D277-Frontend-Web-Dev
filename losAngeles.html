Los Angeles

City of Angeles

Los Angeles

is a sprawling Southern California city and the center of the nation's film and television industry. Near its iconic Hollywood sign, studios such as Paramount Pictures, Universal and Warner Brothers offer behind-the-scenes tours. On Hollywood Boulevard, TCL Chinese Theatre displays celebrities' hand- and footprints, the Walk of Fame honors thousands of luminaries and vendors sell maps to stars' homes.

Quick Facts

  • Population:

    3.821 million

  • Incorporated:

    April 4, 1850

  • Region:

    Southern California

  • Classification:

    Urban

  • Median Household Income:

    $55,870 (State Median: $95,521)

Landmark & Attraction

Hollywood Sign, a globally recognized symbol of the entertainment industry.

History & Heritage

Los Angeles grew rapidly during the early 20th century, fueled by the oil and film industries.

Neighborhood Highlight

Venice Beach, known for its bohemian spirit, Muscle Beach, and vibrant boardwalk.

Local Cuisine & Dining

Los Angeles is renowned for its diverse food scene, including iconic dishes like the French Dip Sandwich and Korean BBQ.

Sport Team

Los Angeles Lakers, the city's legendary NBA team with a rich history of championships.

Famous Person

Kobe Bryant, the iconic basketball player who made an indelible mark on the city and its sports culture.
