San Diego

America's Finest City

San Diego

is a city on the Pacific coast of California known for its beaches, parks and warm climate. Immense Balboa Park is the site of the renowned San Diego Zoo, as well as numerous art galleries, artist studios, museums and gardens. A deep harbor is home to a large active naval fleet, with the USS Midway, an aircraft-carrier-turned-museum, open to the public.

Quick Facts

  • Population:

    1.388 million

  • Incorporated:

    March 27, 1850

  • Region:

    Southern California

  • Classification:

    Urban

  • Median Household Income:

    $63,996 (State Median: $95,521)

Landmark & Attraction

Balboa Park, a sprawling urban cultural park with museums, gardens, and the San Diego Zoo.

History & Heritage

San Diego is one of the oldest towns in California, founded as a mission in 1769.

Neighborhood Highlight

La Jolla, known for its stunning coastline, upscale shops, and beautiful beaches.

Local Cuisine & Dining

San Diego is famous for its fish tacos, a delicious blend of local seafood and Mexican flavors.

Sport Team

San Diego Padres, the city's MLB team that plays at the beautiful Petco Park.

Famous Person

Tony Hawk, the legendary skateboarder who grew up in San Diego and revolutionized the sport.
